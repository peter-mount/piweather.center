The windchill function calculates the windchill based on the temperature and wind speed. If the values passed are not of the correct type then this will return an error.

This function is only valid for temperatures below 10°C (50°F) and for wind faster than 1.34m/s. As such the feelslike function may be more suitable as it's response will be dependent on the temperature and wind speed passed to it.

Syntax

weathercalc, weatherdb

windchill( temperature, windSpeed )

go

Definition

The Wind Chill Index used is that implemented by the United Kingdom, Canada and the United States in November 2001.

It is determined by iterating a model of skin temperature under various wind speeds and temperatures using standard engineering correlations of wind speed and heat transfer rate. Heat transfer was calculated for a bare face in wind, facing the wind, while walking into it at 1.4 m/s (5.0 km/h; 3.1 mph). The model corrects the officially measured wind speed to the wind speed at face height, assuming the person is in an open field.

The results of this model may be approximated, to within one degree, from the following equation:

TWC = 35.74 + 0.6215T - 35.75 v0.16 + 0.4275 T v0.16

Where:

  • T is the ambient dry bulb temperature (°F)
  • v the wind speed in m/s.
